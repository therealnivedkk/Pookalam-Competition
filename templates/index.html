What is Pookalam?

Pookalam is a beautiful floral carpet made from fresh flowers, traditionally created during the festival of Onam in Kerala. Teams compete by designing intricate and colorful patterns on the ground using a variety of flowers, showcasing creativity, symmetry, and cultural pride. It is one of the most cherished traditions of Onam celebrations.

How the Competition Works

📝
Step 1
Register

Teams register with their college name, member details, and chosen theme for their Pookalam design.

🌺
Step 2
Design

Teams create their Pookalam using fresh flowers and upload a photo of their completed design for judging.

⭐
Step 3
Judging

Certified judges evaluate each Pookalam on three criteria — Symmetry, Color Harmony, and Variety of Flowers — each scored out of 10.

🏆
Step 4
Results

Scores from all judges are aggregated in real time. The Live Leaderboard ranks teams by their total score.

Scoring Criteria

🔄
Symmetry

How balanced and proportional is the design? Judges look for perfect alignment and geometric precision.

Out of 10
🎨
Color Harmony

How well do the colors blend together? Judges evaluate the use of contrasting and complementary flower colors.

Out of 10
🌸
Variety of Flowers

How many different types of flowers are used? Greater variety shows creativity and effort.

Out of 10

Maximum Score: 30 points per judge
